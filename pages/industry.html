Ideal Job: Data Specialist
Labour Market Assessment: No Shortage
Future Demand: Moderate Future Demand

Small and large businesses alike are slowly increasing the demand for data specialists to analyse data to increase efficiency within their business. Even though each business and organisation all require to have at least one data specialist within their data analysis team, this reflects why there is not as much of a shortage even though they are in demand from employers. This due to a rise in popularity for the role as majority of businesses cannot clean and visualise the data like an experienced data specialist can. This ensures their demand going forward into the future even with AI technology around the corner because this role must not only understand data, but they also need to understand the people they are working with to get the best results from presenting their data to their employers.

Why this job?

"This role appeals to me because I like to take time to organise things into their respective places and I take a great deal of personal and professional pride in this fact. As a government job, it also offers a lot of stability which is important as I progress my career and wish to start a family. I also have an interest in data analytics and as this role also draws on my previous experience in management, it is a position I would have some comfort going into."

After Burning Glass Data Analysis

"Although there is not a high demand for this role, it is still moderately sought after and as such my opinion has not changed. It is a role that will always be required in some form, especially approaching things like the Census. The fact that there is so much security in a government role such as thins really cements it as an ideal choice for me."

Ideal Job: Cyber Security Analyst
Labour Market Assessment: Shortage
Future Demand: Strong Future Demand

Businesses and Organisations are becoming more aware of how important Cybersecurity is to their critical infrastructure. There is at least one cyber security analyst in a business or in some instances an entire Cybersecurity team, this creates multiple jobs for the role. Due to education requirements combined with established experience many struggle to enter the Pure-Cybersecurity space, and many get forced into hybrid jobs such as network engineer or project management which in-turn they then settle on as a career as they are highly paid regardless and do not require extensive further education and work experience. All of these are major factors of shortage in Cybersecurity jobs.

With Cybersecurity already having fewer candidates than most IT jobs this only increases the demand more especially as technology evolves and we see more use of cloud computing and the Internet of Things the demand for Cybersecurity Analysts will only grow.

Why this job?

"he role of Senior Cyber Security Analyst is appealing to me as you are both a part of the blue and red team when it comes to protecting and serving the community or even the country. Being a part of the Cyber Security team in a hospital is a noticeably big deal as you have hundreds of lives in your hands every day, it might not seem like it, but one attack can cause great chaos, panic, and doubt within a community. This was the case throughout the pandemic in early 2020 having black hat organisations attempt to steal patient information such as social security numbers in America or using ransomware to extort them and disable systems, putting patients' lives at risk just for some examples. It does not have to be for St Vincent's Hospital but working as Cyber Security for the Government whether it be subcontracted or employed directly is the ideal job for me."

After Burning Glass Data Analysis

"Looking at this just confirms the growth and cost cyber security has cost multiple countries over a few years and it will only increase as technology and methods improve. I have spoken with a renown cyber security expert who has given me an advanced guide for my pathway into an entry level position and how to further career progression which gives me the confidence needed to pursue this as a career. AI technology is the only fear I had but upon further research and my understanding is that “hackers” can foil the AI algorithms quite easily which will increase the threat landscape."

After looking at more information and studies, particularly “RMIT why we need hybrid jobs”:

While the IT industry is full of tech-based jobs, all these jobs are hybrid jobs like the ones mentioned above. The fact these roles require multiple skill sets and being able to do more than one job, this is what secures them as a future role going forward and only increases the demand even with the fear of future technology like job automation. Looking at studies they show 42% of jobs can be automated in the future while only 12% of hybrid jobs will truly be automated. Therefore, being able to understand of how to enable all three of these foundational skill groups is what creates your success when working in any field within the IT industry and will ensure your longevity in any role. Which is why IT professions have such a broad skillset range.

Comparing our Ideal Jobs with common skill sets

Data specialists and Senior analysts share the many of the foundational skills of digital building blocks, these typically statistical programming languages and other office applications. The core programming languages used are Python, R and SQL, these allow analysts to compare data sets at a large scale with the use of algorithms and machine learning. All this raw data is useless without creating a visualisation that is easy to read and understand for their employer, this is where the use of Microsoft 365 (Excel, PowerPoint, Word), PowerBI and Salesforce come into play.

Network Administrators are primarily built upon the digital building blocks of digital security, network administration and management, hardware management, system administration and virtualisation. Being a Network Administrator means to be an expert of both digital and physical infrastructure of their employer with the type of operating systems used at business. They tend to specialise in programming languages like Python, PERL, and TCL.

Cloud Engineers and Cyber Security Analysts are built the same foundational skills of digital security, networking, and virtualisation but need to know not only how a network works but also be experts in their retrospective field. Cloud Engineers for instance will have a wide range of programming languages at their disposal such as .NET, Golong, PHP, Python and SQL. They also need to completely understand cloud computing and infrastructure, all the different cloud service providers such as AWS, Azure and GoogleGo, while improving their digital security skills to fit their role typically with blockchain, cloud and mobile operating system security. Cyber Security Analysts need to understand the foundations of both roles but have a more advanced knowledge of all operating systems along with a vast knowledge of programming languages like C, C++, Java, JavaScript, PowerShell, and Python. Understanding a large variety of programming languages compliments the role further allowing them to work on a wide range of projects with any type of security asset.

App Developers while specialising in being software developers with a passion for creation of mobile applications they still need to have a complete understanding of how to use a computer much like any other IT role as applications typically developed using these programming languages Java, JavaScript, Kotlin, Swift, C#. With the advance in windows and now being able to code with Swift (used to create iOS applications) App developers are free to use whatever device has their preferred operating system. Having a complete understanding of cloud computing, networks and operating systems allows for a lot more functionality with apps as technology is evolving customers and employers are excepting more features such as complete cross-platform capabilities, cloud storage for files or game saves and an easy-to-use user interface and better user experience.

Whether you want to be a Data Specialist, Senior Analyst, Network Administrator, Cloud Engineer, Cyber Security Analyst or even an App Developer all of these require a broad range of required skills, whether that be digital building blocks, human skills or business enabling skills. All these jobs need to well-rounded with their analytical skills, critical thinking, and problem-solving skills. Being able to analyse and think on your feet is not enough though, the ability to collaborate as part of a team and work with others either in your field, or across the room in a meeting is crucial for success in the IT industry. These all a necessity for any job, these skills ensure that the IT professional can think clearly and rationally while understanding the information they are looking at whether it be code for programming, network intrusion or statistical data, and then the ability to convey this information in an appropriate manner to team members or superiors. As if this is not enough... Any job in IT will typically have you doing more than one job at time, having skills like being able to multi-task along with organisational skills and time management are three other crucial human skills for all IT roles to have. All these human skills are the basic foundational skills anyone wanting to work in the IT industry will want to develop to the best of their ability to succeed.

Having all these flashy IT skills and human skills can be completely worthless if you do not understand how to enable them within a business or workplace setting. The primary focus of all these jobs is accomplished by understanding how to use business enabler skills such as, business analysis and processes, communication of data and digital design, project management and risk mitigation. These all assist in business for growth and improvement, with skills to analyse the companies they work for and provide tailored solutions with their skill set. As these jobs progress and you get promoted to a senior position being proficient in project management is also necessary as you will be managing projects and team members.

The foundation of digital building blocks separates what defines your job in an IT role. As the IT industry is full of hybrid jobs, meaning you take on more than one job at a time, the perquisite for success is to have all the human and business enabler skills mentioned above, to create deeper connections with colleagues and customers. Even though all our jobs differ in what area we want to specialise in, to get a job in an advanced IT role these skills are all a must have to succeed no matter what job you want to go into.
